Category & Callsigns
Category 2A (Two Alpha)
Callsigns:
Main stations: VE3LCA                                  (Victor Echo Three Lima Charlie Alpha)
GOTA station: VE3WCC                               (Victor Echo Three Whiskey Charlie Charlie)
Field Day Categories
Number indicates # of HF stations.
Letter indicates source of power and operators:
A: Club or Non-Club portable, emergency power
B: 1 or 2 person portable, emergency power
C: Mobile stations (includes Aeronautical and Maritime Mobile)
D: Home stations, normal power
E: Home stations, emergency power
F: Emergency Operations Centers
Rules
Only 2 HF signals plus GOTA station, satellite station and VHF/UHF station
Max 150 watts
No WARC bands
Cant operate and later work us from home
No repeaters allowed
Each phone, cw and digital band segment considered a separate band
May work a station once per band

Safety
Safety is Paramount!
Recommended safety equipment:
Work gloves
Hard hats
Sturdy boots
Sunhat and sunblock
Water bottle
Lightning
Lightning kills 73 North Americans annually
Our site high and clear  caution required!
We will follow 30-30 rule
Safe area is inside metal vehicle
What to do if about to be struck
What to do if someone is struck
Fire Safety
No smoking in tents or near generators
Stop generators before refueling
Fire extinguishers near generators, cooking location and operating positions
Bring shovels in case of grass fire
Electrical Safety
Proper extension cords
Waterproof all connections
Kill power before troubleshooting
Use proper grounds
RF safety
Dont stick tongue in light socket while standing in puddle during an electrical storm
Tower Safety
Proper equipment a must
Use caution putting up towers
Dont poke an eye on an antenna
Ensure guys are securely anchored
Use marking tape to indicate guys
NO Vehicles allowed near towers
If necessary, let tower fall  but you get out of way!

Operating Notes
Keep an eye on 10 and 15M
Listen to bands before Field Day
Once contact made on HF band, must stay on same band for at least 15 minutes
Read rules carefully
Practice using logging program beforehand
Ensure computer date & time correct before contest starts!
Operating Notes 2
On 6M, CW 50.110, SSB 50.125 and up
On 2M, CW 144.1 up, SSB 144.2 and up
On 70cm, CW & SSB 432.100 and up
Dont forget FM (not calling frequencies!)
Avoid 52.525, 146.52, 446.0 MHz (but operate nearby!)
Vertical polarization for FM
Horizontal polarization for CW/SSB
Grid FN15
