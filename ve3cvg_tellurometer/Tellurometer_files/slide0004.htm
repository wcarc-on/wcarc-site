How it Works as an Instrument
•Main frequency is determined from the tuning of the Klystron Cavity
•A continuous wave in the range of 10,000-10,500MHz is radiated from MASTER unit
•The wave is fm modulated by pattern freq of 7.5MHz or 10MHz
•Wave received at REMOTE is re-radiated to MASTER
•Phase difference of received modulation is a measure of the transit TIME of wave over the path
•Changing frequencies a measured amount, increases the accuracy
•With a pattern freq of 7.492377MHz, one phase rotation represents 40m of the 2 way path or 20m between units
•Distance = velocity factor X one way transit time
•Transit time is computed by the instrument
•Accurate velocity factor comes from 299792.5/refractive index = km/s
•Refractive Index is about 1.000325 but depends on temperature and humidity
