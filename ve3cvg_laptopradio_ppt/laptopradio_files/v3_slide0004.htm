802.11b & 802.11g
• 14 overlapping, staggered, channels with center frequencies 5 MHz
apart
• 802.11b signal must be attenuated by at least 30 dB from its peak
energy at ±11 MHz from the center frequency, and at least 50 dB from
its peak energy at ±22 MHz
• 802.11b throughput is about 11mbit/s with fallback based on signal
strength
• 802.11g throughput is 54mbit/s. Similar to 802.11a (on 5.8GHz). Uses
orthogonal frequency division multiplexing (OFDM)
• 802.11b is compatible with 802.11g but slows it down.
