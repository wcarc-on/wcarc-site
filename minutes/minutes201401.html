Opening:
The meeting was opened by President Clayton Smith (VE3IRR) at 19:30 hours.


Secretary's Report:
The Secretary introduced the Minutes of the November meeting (Web Master's Note: This was corrected to read 'the December Meeting' at the February 2014 Regular Meeting). Moved by Glenn MacDonell (VE3XRA) that the Minutes, as corrected, be approved. Seconded by Ken (VE3KA).
Motion approved.


Treasurer's Report:
No report as the Treasurer was not present.


Vice President's Report:
The Vice President position was not filled at the Annuual General Meeting in December. The President is carrying out those duties for the time being. He reported that the regular meeting room is booked for the next two months and will be booked for the following month shortly. He noted that speakers for these meetings had not been booked yet and invited members to report on interesting projects they are undertaking.


President's Report:
The President reported that he had completed the addition to the annual report agreed on at the previous meeting. The annual report can be found on the club website in the Club History section.
He recalled that he had promised following his September presentation to post software designed radio (SDR) examples to go along with his talk. He had already emailed several examples and he noted he had recently posted several more examples and would soon send an email to the WCARC list with the link to these examples. Old Business:
The President noted that there was a vacancy for the position of Vice President and called for nominations. As the position was not filled he will again call for nominations at the next meeting.

Reports

Beacons
Doug Leach (VE3XK) reported that all beacons were working. He also advised members that the earlier discussed possibility of relocating the beacons to a tower at the Diefenbunker site seemed increasingly remote as the proposed tower project at the Diefenbunker was not moving forward at present. The beacons work well at the present site. Although a higher site would have some advantages it appears that they will stay at their present location for the foreseeable future. This would mean that planned exenditures to move the beacons would not likely be made this year. Dave Goodwin (VE3AAQ) mentioned another possible site and agreed to investigate further and report back at the next meeting.

2304 MHz Transverter Conversion
Bert (VE2ZAZ) asked if members participating in the conversion project had any questions based on their work to date. He also offered to show his completed transverter, if requested. He suggested that if some paticipants did not have the required test equipment for some operations such as maximizing power output it could be possible for him to bring the equipment so that this work could be done after a meeting.

Ski Marathon
Harold VA3UNK informed members that the Canadian Ski Marathon would take place Feb 8. This year participants will ski from West to East: the first day from Buckingham to Montebello and the second from Montebello to Lachute. This is the 41st year that radio amateurs have provided communications for the event. He is still looking for radio amateurs to volunteer. Participation for a single day or the entire event is possible. Further information can be found at www.radio-1.ca. For anyone unable to access the website or having questions not answered there they can contact Harold at va3unk@rac.ca.

Presentation:
Bert (VE2ZAZ) gave us an interesting talk on VoIP, Asterisk-PBX and the Raspberry Pi.

Adjournment
The President, Clayton (VE3IRR) stated that there being no further business, the meeting was adjourned at 21:10 hrs.
